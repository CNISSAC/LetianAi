Robotic-Assisted Medical Instrument Planning and Execution using Mixed Reality

Robotic-assisted medical systems (RAMS) have gained significant attention for their advantages in alleviating clinician fatigue and improving patient outcomes. These systems encompass a range of human-robot interactions, including medical scene monitoring, anatomy target planning, and robot manipulation. However, despite their versatility and effective- ness, RAMS demand a high level of expertise from operators, creating a significant barrier between medical staff and the robotic system. In this work, we introduce a novel frame- work that provides informative and user-friendly interactions using mixed reality technologies to improve the interface and ease the use of RAMS. The proposed framework achieves on-the-fly instrument planning and execution by providing anatomic image overlay, avatar-based collision detection, and robot programming interface.
